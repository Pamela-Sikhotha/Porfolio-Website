About Me

I am an ADA graduate who loves all things tech and digital, from designing websites to developing them. My research interest involves user experience, creativity, and graphic design. I have a passion for software development, I have been formally trained and invested time into learning different programming languages such as HTML, CSS, and JavaScript at ADA. I have been able to quickly develop my skills through the help of my lecturers, self-study, and leveraging resources. I have worked on different projects and cannot wait to tackle my next one. I am curious and want to learn and implement new technologies. I have the ability to think creatively, analytically, and with depth about problems.

Community is incredibly important to me. I love working with others and care about constructive feedback since it's an important part of working with people and I am able to both give and receive feedback. I am a young, open-minded woman who strives to learn, grow, and who has the ability to work through obstacles despite setbacks and failures like now experiencing the COVID-19 pandemic at hand since it deterred plans for the future but I continue to be positive. I have the ability to adapt to changing situations and cope with stress, I am also adept at clearly communicating my needs and asking for help. I am always willing to commit myself to all aspects of software development as well as embrace the values of community, inclusivity, and lifelong learning.

I have acquired social and life skills; communication, time management, and independent learning. I am capable to complete a product life cycle from conception to delivery and I can utilize agile practices learned to assist in project completion. I have an insatiable desire to develop and grow my coding skills. Software development is exciting and challenging but I’m ready and excited to pursue my goals. I have so many ideas I would like to use my skills but my first priority is that I hope to inspire others to achieve their dreams regardless of whether or not they look-the-part. It’s important for people to have role models that look more like them in positions that they aspire to be in. I hope to show aspiring developers that despite the current demographic in STEM fields, they should not be deterred in their ambitions.
